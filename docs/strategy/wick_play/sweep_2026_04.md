# Wick Play — Parameter Sweep Report (2026-04)

> Full-grid parameter sweep of the Wick Play multi-ticker strategy against 500 Nasdaq common-stock tickers over 2024-01-01 → 2026-04-22. Raw artifacts in [`backtester/sweep_results/20260423_140117/`](../../../backtester/sweep_results/20260423_140117).

---

## 1. Motivation

Live-traded Multi Wick Play produced **4 trades / +1.8% over ~22 months** on a small Nasdaq-100 slice. Two of the four losses (SHOP 2024-12-18 FOMC "hawkish cut", REGN 2025-02 tariff-shock) correlated with index-wide macro events, not with intrinsic setup failure.

That triggered the following hypotheses:

1. **Index-regime filter** (SPY vs SMA/EMA/short-term drawdown) should neutralize macro-driven losers.
2. **Macro-event blackout** (FOMC, CPI) should eliminate stop-outs that happen *because* of the release, not because of the setup.
3. **EMA trail / min-trail-bars** may be cutting winners prematurely — the only big winner in the live sample (MSFT +12%) only ran because the time stop happened to delay the trail.
4. **Tighter entry (psych_score=4)** is intuitively better but probably too selective.

The sweep is an ablation across these axes simultaneously so the marginal effect of each is readable.

Average `total_return_pct` across every other combination held equal:

  sma50+dd5_2pct  → −1.91%  ┘

**Counterintuitive finding**: `cpi_w1` out-performs every FOMC variant in this window. The 2024-12-18 FOMC was the obvious bogeyman ex-ante, but in aggregate CPI-release volatility did more damage to Wick Play breakouts than FOMC days did.

## 6. Exit-mechanism stats (6,866 trades, all configs)

| Exit reason | count | share | avg R | win rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `exhaustion_exit` | 390 | 5.7% | **+1.74** | **100%** |
| `ema_trail` | 4,628 | 67.4% | +0.15 | 48.6% |
| `wick_low_stop` | 1,608 | 23.4% | −1.08 | 0% |
| `end_of_data` | 240 | 3.5% | −0.32 | 0% |

- The **Exhaustion Extension Top** counter-detector — Kell's own pairing — is where the asymmetric edge lives. 390 out of 6,866 trades (5.7%) is the pay-off tail.
- `ema_trail` is the workhorse but roughly break-even per trade (+0.15R). It's carrying capital, not alpha.
- `wick_low_stop` is well-behaved: exactly 0% win, avg −1.08R (minor slippage over the −1R wick-low).

**Implication**: further tuning should protect the exhaustion path (don't trail-out the trade before Exhaustion Extension Top can fire). The `min_trail_bars=5` change supports this — it forces a 5-bar window where neither EMA-trail nor exhaustion checks fire, so the trade has time to develop.

## 9. Open questions / caveats

1. **Window-specific**: Only 2.3 years, 500 tickers, no regime variety (2024-2026 is a bull with two notable shocks). The CPI-over-FOMC finding may invert in a different regime. Rerun annually.
2. **Top-tier ties**: 6 configs tie at +4.44% because several regime filters reject the same dates. To break the tie, re-sweep on a larger window (2020-2026) or with a more adversarial regime (2022).
3. **Profit factor 1.21 is thin**. Strategy edge is real but small — any additional fee load or slippage assumption erodes it.
4. **Exhaustion exits carry the tail**. Before changing anything upstream (entry filters), study the 390 exhaustion trades in `trades.parquet` to understand what characterises the setup that produces them.
5. **We didn't sweep stop_mode** (wick_low vs inside_low). Kept at `wick_low` per Kell's preference. Inside_low would likely raise win rate but reduce avg winner — needs separate sweep.
6. **Psych_score=4 collapses the sample**. Any entry-tightening dimension (tighter wick ratio, higher breakout strength) likely does the same. Edge is fragile to over-filtering.

# Follow-up — Exit-focused sweep on S&P 500 2017-2025

> Artifacts: [`backtester/sweep_results/exit_20260423_154205/`](../../../backtester/sweep_results/exit_20260423_154205). Script: [`sweep_wickplay_exit.py`](../../../backtester/sweep_wickplay_exit.py).

## 12. Why a second sweep

A user-supplied S&P 500 export (2017-2025, 73 trades, +$75.9K net, PF 1.36) made two things stark:

1. **Exhaustion Extension Top is the sole alpha source**: 7/73 trades (9.6%), **100% win rate**, +$127.6K total. Strip those and the strategy net-zero's.
2. **Wick Low Stop is the bleed**: 17/73 trades (23%), 0% win, **−$145.1K** — almost perfectly cancels the Exhaustion gain.
3. The first sweep (Nasdaq 2024-2026) never varied the exit parameters, so the `extension_atr_mult=2.5` default was fossilized despite only producing 7 Exhaustion hits over 9 years.

Goal of this follow-up: keep Kell's canonical recipe untouched (3-bar structure, wick-low stop, 10 EMA trail, Exhaustion pair), tune ONLY:
- `extension_atr_mult` (how extended above slow-EMA before we call it a top)
- `min_slow_slope` (is the uptrend slope at least this positive)
- Breakeven stop ON/OFF and its arm / offset multiples

## 13. Setup

| Universe | `sp500` (503 tickers, Wikipedia) |

## 15. Dimension effects

### `extension_atr_mult` — U-shape (sweet spot at 2.2)

| ext | avg_return | avg exh_exits | avg MaxDD | max return |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1.5 | +31.2% | 26.75 | 10.70% | +33.8% |
| 1.8 | +38.6% | 18.75 | 11.28% | +39.6% |
| 2.0 | +38.0% | 15.00 | 11.32% | +38.2% |
| **2.2** | **+44.1%** | **13.00** | **10.20%** | **+44.93%** |
| 2.5 (old) | +32.1% | 5.00 | 13.17% | +34.0% |

Both extremes lose return. `ext=2.5` (the previous default) only fires Exhaustion 5 times in 9 years — you're **never taking the profit-lock exit that defines the pattern**. `ext=1.5` fires 27 times but many prematurely, cutting winners mid-run.

### Breakeven — `arm=2.0 / offset=1.0` wins

| breakeven | avg_return | avg win_rate | avg wstop | avg be_ex | avg DD |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| off | +36.5% | 45.2% | 16.8 | 0.0 | 11.3% |
| arm1.0_off0.0 | +36.6% | **40.5%** | 16.2 | 6.2 | 12.2% |
| arm1.5_off0.5 | +36.9% | 45.5% | 16.8 | 3.8 | 11.0% |
| **arm2.0_off1.0** | **+37.2%** | 45.5% | 16.8 | 2.0 | **10.9%** |

`arm1.0_off0.0` (pure breakeven) is **a trap** — it trips on routine pullbacks *before* winners can fully develop, collapsing win rate from 45% → 40%. The conservative `arm2.0_off1.0` only fires 2 times on average but those are the cases where a +2R winner reversed; locking +1R instead of giving back is the marginal contribution.

### `min_slow_slope` — no effect

0.000, 0.005, 0.010 produce essentially identical aggregates (+36.7%-ish). The slope gate can be dropped.

## 17. Canonical-preservation check

These changes tune **two existing parameters** and **turn one existing opt-in ON**. They do not alter:

- 3-bar structural detection
- Wick-low stop placement
- 10-EMA trailing logic
- Exhaustion Extension Top as the pair exit

So the "정석적 Wick Play" recipe is intact. The edge added is **tuning exhaustion to be actually discoverable** (dropped from 2.5σ to 2.2σ extension) and **protecting +2R winners with a +1R lock**.

## 18. Residual open questions

1. **Still leaning on a few fat winners**. Top 5 trades in config #39 carry ~60% of PnL. The ±10% MaxDD is comforting but reflects single-position sizing — a correlated drawdown across multiple strategies would be worse.
2. **Why doesn't looser slope (0.0) beat stricter (0.01)?** Slope is basically noise in this universe. Worth revisiting on a regime-heterogeneous window.
3. **`arm=2.0 / offset=1.0` fires only 2 times avg per 9-year run.** Effect is real but the sample is small. Rerun on a larger universe + window to rule out overfit.
4. **S&P 500 vs Nasdaq comparison**: the earlier sweep on Nasdaq found `ext` didn't move (wasn't in the grid). A combined sweep (`ext` × `mtb` × `regime` × `macro`) on both universes would be the cleanest next step — but it's ~600 configs × 15 min each, so budget ≈ 2.5 hrs.
